# Evolution and De-Evolution Master Reference

Source: Jeff Lyons, Rapid Story Development (Routledge, 2020).

## The Two Circuits

### Hexad Circuit (1, 4, 2, 8, 5, 7)

Six styles connected by internal lines on the Enneagram diagram.

**Safety direction (evolution)**:
- 1 → 7 (Relax, appreciate beauty, let go of rules)
- 7 → 5 (Think deeply, savor solitude, slow down)
- 5 → 8 (Visible, assertive, bring knowledge to world)
- 8 → 2 (Vulnerable, giving, helping others)
- 2 → 4 (Full emotional palette, creative impulses)
- 4 → 1 (Focus outward, self-discipline, create something special)

**Fear direction (de-evolution)**:
- 1 → 4 (Fantasy, micromanaging chaos)
- 4 → 2 (Needy, desperate for connection)
- 2 → 8 (Controlling, forceful, dominating)
- 8 → 5 (Withdraw, paranoid, go it alone)
- 5 → 7 (Abandon thinking, numb with excess)
- 7 → 1 (Critical, righteous, perfectionist)

### Triad Circuit (3, 6, 9)

The equilateral triangle connecting these three styles.

**Safety direction (evolution)**:
- 3 → 6 (Achieve for others' safety, not approval)
- 6 → 9 (Peace, relaxation, enjoy work)
- 9 → 3 (Personal presence, achievement, productive)

**Fear direction (de-evolution)**:
- 3 → 9 (Lethargy, resentment, no value)
- 9 → 6 (Frozen in fear, worst-case paralysis)
- 6 → 3 (Desperate for a win, resentful)

## How to Use This in Story Architecture

**Pattern of Decline**: The protagonist moves from their core type TOWARD their de-evolution point through the middle. Each pass through the offer-refusal loop pushes them further along the fear direction. The decline should be visible in specific behavioral changes consistent with the de-evolution type's worst qualities.

**Pattern of Elevation**: After the doom moment, the protagonist begins to access their evolution point. The seven elevation steps track movement from rock bottom toward the safety direction. The first step toward change should show behavior consistent with the evolution type's best qualities.

**Opponent Profile**: The main opponent should embody the WORST qualities of the protagonist's de-evolution type. Read the full profile for that type and pull from its negative traits, communication patterns, and pinch behaviors to build the opponent.

**Protagonist Change Triangle**:
- Start Statement: Protagonist at their core type (survival strategy + core image in action)
- Evolution Statement (+): Protagonist accessing evolution point qualities
- De-Evolution Statement (-): Protagonist at their worst, exhibiting de-evolution point behaviors

The triangle is NOT a simple good/bad binary. The evolution path requires genuine loss of the survival strategy that has kept the protagonist safe. It's terrifying. The de-evolution path is seductive because it feels like a more extreme version of what already "works."
