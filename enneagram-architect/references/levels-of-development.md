# Levels of Development

Each type has nine levels ranging from healthy (1-3) through average (4-6) to unhealthy (7-9). Use these to identify where a character currently sits and track their arc movement.

---

## Type One Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Wise, Accepting | Lets go of need to judge. Achieves true integrity by accepting imperfection. Noble, discerning, hopeful. |
| 2 | Reasonable | Focused on conscience and reason. Self-image: "I am sensible, moderate, objective." Guided by internal values. |
| 3 | Principled | Highly ethical, self-disciplined. Teaches by example. Puts aside personal desires for greater good. |
| 4 | Striving | Fears others don't share principles. Becomes serious, driven. Debates and corrects. Evaluates everything. |
| 5 | Orderly | Worries about condemnation. Rigidly organizes everything. Punctual, methodical, but irritable and tense. |
| 6 | Critical | Afraid others will mess up their order. Angry no one takes ideals seriously. Perfectionistic, sarcastic. |
| 7 | Inflexible | Fears ideals may be wrong. Self-righteous, closed-minded. No compromises. Bitter, misanthropic. |
| 8 | Obsessive | Desperate to control irrational impulses. Acts out desires while publicly condemning them. |
| 9 | Punitive | Realizes they've become what they hate. Self-destructive. Punishes self or others violently. |

## Type Two Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Unconditional | Loves without expectation. Joyous, gracious, humble. Receives love freely. |
| 2 | Caring | Empathetic, compassionate, sincere. Self-image: "I am loving, thoughtful, selfless." |
| 3 | Giving | Generous with time/energy. Encouraging. Takes care of self too. Empowers rather than creates dependency. |
| 4 | People-Pleasing | Fears not being enough. Cultivates friendships through flattery. Seductive attention. |
| 5 | Possessive | Fears being replaced. Puts others' needs first to create obligation. Won't let others out of sight. |
| 6 | Overbearing | Angry at being taken for granted. Complains about health, reminds of sacrifices. Martyrdom. |
| 7 | Manipulative | Fears driving people away. Instills guilt. Keeps others dependent. Rationalizes as others' fault. |
| 8 | Coercive | Desperate for love. Feels entitled. Acts out need for affection recklessly. Won't take no. |
| 9 | Victimized | Feels abused by everyone. Chronic health problems. Falls apart to punish others. |

## Type Three Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Authentic | Discovers value isn't dependent on achievement. Inner-directed, genuine, self-accepting, benevolent. |
| 2 | Admirable | Adaptable, charming, high self-esteem. Self-image: "I am outstanding, capable, well-adjusted." |
| 3 | Self-Improving | Develops talents earnestly. Competent, confident, exemplary. Inspires through genuine achievement. |
| 4 | Performing | Fears being overshadowed. Drives relentlessly. Compares to others. Career becomes everything. |
| 5 | Image-Conscious | Worries about perception. Packages self to expectations. Problems with intimacy. Smooth facade. |
| 6 | Grandiose | Fears no one notices without huge success. Self-promoting, arrogant. Secret neediness underneath. |
| 7 | Deceptive | Fears failing. Deceives self and others. Says whatever impresses. Empty and depressed inside. |
| 8 | Opportunistic | Desperate for attention. Concocts stories. Maliciously sabotages others' success. |
| 9 | Relentless | Nothing wins needed approval. Loses control of hostility. Destroys what reminds them of failure. |

## Type Four Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Life-Embracing | Free from self-absorption. Self-renewing, redemptive, revelatory. Creates from joy, not pain. |
| 2 | Sensitive | Introspective, self-aware, gentle, tactful. Self-image: "I am sensitive, different, self-aware." |
| 3 | Creative | Expresses individuality through creative action. Eloquent, subtle. Universal through personal. |
| 4 | Romanticizing | Fears feelings won't sustain creativity. Uses imagination to prolong moods. Dreams of rescue. |
| 5 | Self-Absorbed | Worries others won't appreciate uniqueness. Aloof, melancholy. Fragility as armor. |
| 6 | Self-Indulgent | Fears demands will destroy dreams. Envies others. Exempts self from rules. Pretentious, unproductive. |
| 7 | Alienated | Fears wasting life. Rejects everything. Depressed, fatigued, apathetic. Burns every bridge. |
| 8 | Self-Rejecting | Hates self. Loathes everything that doesn't match fantasy. Blames others for not saving them. |
| 9 | Despairing | Realizes wasted life on futile fantasies. Self-destructive. May attempt to elicit rescue through harm. |

## Type Five Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Visionary | Engages confidently with world. Clear-minded, knowing, profound, compassionate. Transforms through gifts. |
| 2 | Perceptive | Observant, insightful, extraordinary concentration. Self-image: "I am smart, curious, independent." |
| 3 | Innovative | Masters knowledge/skills. Pioneering thinker. Independent and inventive. |
| 4 | Conceptualizing | Fears skills insufficient. Prepares endlessly. Studies, practices, collects. Prefers theory to action. |
| 5 | Detached | Worries others' needs will distract. Shuts out "intrusions." Minimizes needs. Cerebral, secretive. |
| 6 | Provocative | Fears threats to niche. Tries to fend people off. Enjoys subverting beliefs. Bizarre, scornful. |
| 7 | Eccentric | Fears incapable of finding place. Cuts off connections. Empty, nihilistic. |
| 8 | Horrified | Everything ominous. Dark fantasies, strange perceptions. Resists help. Shrinks from everyone. |
| 9 | Self-Annihilating | Can't defend against terror. Seeks oblivion. Psychotic breaks possible. |

## Type Six Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Courageous | Discovers inner guidance. Self-reliant. Truly secure, grounded, serene, valiant. |
| 2 | Reliable | Engaging, trustworthy. Builds connection/stability. Self-image: "I am solid, attentive, dependable." |
| 3 | Cooperative | Creates mutually beneficial systems. Well-disciplined, practical. Foresees problems. |
| 4 | Dutiful | Fears losing independence while needing support. Invests uneasily. Seeks reassurance. |
| 5 | Ambivalent | Worries about conflicting demands. Anxious, pessimistic, suspicious. Indecisive. |
| 6 | Authoritarian | Fears losing support, unsure of self. Blames others. Power struggles. |
| 7 | Panicky | Fears actions harmed security. Depressed, helpless. Looks for salvation. |
| 8 | Paranoid | Believes others will destroy safety. Strikes at real or imagined enemies. |
| 9 | Self-Destructive | Guilt and self-hatred. Brings down everything built. May invite disgrace. |

## Type Seven Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Satisfied | Lets go of need for more. Fully assimilates experiences. Appreciative, ecstatic, grateful. |
| 2 | Enthusiastic | Excited by possibilities. Lively, spontaneous, resilient. Self-image: "I am happy, spontaneous, outgoing." |
| 3 | Productive | Fully engages with life. Prolific, practical. Optimistic, accomplished. Actually finishes things. |
| 4 | Consuming | Fears missing out. Restless for more. Chases trends. Can't enjoy present for planning future. |
| 5 | Scattered | Worries about boredom. Keeps energy high. Distracted, unfocused. Thousand half-finished projects. |
| 6 | Excessive | Fears not enough. Impatient, demanding, seldom satisfied. Jaded, wasteful. |
| 7 | Escaping | Fears actions bringing pain. Panics. Highly impulsive. Pursues anything promising relief. Joyless. |
| 8 | Manic | Desperate to escape. Out of control. Alternates hysterical activity and depression. Reckless. |
| 9 | Paralyzed | Ruined capacity for enjoyment. Panic-stricken, trapped. Health destroyed. |

## Type Eight Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Heroic | Uses strength to help others. Magnanimous, courageous, forgiving. Lays down arms for greater purpose. |
| 2 | Strong | Vigorous, action-oriented, self-reliant. Self-image: "I am assertive, direct, resourceful." |
| 3 | Leading | Takes on challenges. Protects and provides. Strategic, decisive. Fights for their people. |
| 4 | Enterprising | Fears not enough resources. Shrewd, competitive. More guarded emotionally. Always wins but rarely connects. |

| 8 | Megalomaniacal | Desperate to protect self. Attacks preemptively. Delusions of invincibility. |

## Type Nine Levels

| Level | State | Behavioral Markers |
|-------|-------|-------------------|
| 1 | Self-Possessed | Fully awake and present. Dynamic, serene, indomitable. Transforms by being fully present. |
| 2 | Peaceful | Unselfconscious, emotionally stable. Self-image: "I am steady, easygoing, kind." |
| 3 | Comforting | Creates peace and harmony. Patient, levelheaded, imaginative. Inspires with healing vision. |
| 4 | Agreeable | Fears conflict will ruin peace. Goes along. Says yes when means no. Uses platitudes. |
| 5 | Disengaged | Worries changes will disrupt peace. Tunes out problems. Comforting routines. Physically present, emotionally absent. |
| 6 | Resigned | Fears demands create anxiety. Downplays problems. Trudges. Suppresses anger. "Fine" means anything but. |
| 7 | Repressed | Defends illusion everything's okay. Stubbornly resists confronting. Depressed, ineffectual, listless. |
| 8 | Dissociating | Desperate for any peace. Blocks awareness. Numb, helpless, amnesiac. |
| 9 | Disappearing | Unable to face reality. Completely unresponsive. May fragment. |

## Using Levels for Arc Analysis

**Positive Arc:** Character moves UP the levels (e.g., Level 6 → Level 3)
- Look for: Crisis that forces confrontation with blind spot
- Key moment: When they choose connection/truth over defense mechanism

**Negative Arc:** Character moves DOWN the levels (e.g., Level 4 → Level 8)
- Look for: Compromise that rationalizes the next step
- Key moment: When they become what they most feared/despised

**Flat Arc:** Character maintains level while affecting others
- Look for: Consistent behavior that challenges other characters to change

**Tragic Arc:** Character achieves growth but too late, or growth is undone
- Look for: Moment of clarity followed by irreversible consequences
