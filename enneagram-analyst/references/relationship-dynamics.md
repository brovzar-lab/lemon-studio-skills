# Relationship Dynamics

## Opponent Design (from RSD)

The protagonist's de-evolution point provides the template for the ideal opponent.

| Protagonist | De-Evolves To | Ideal Opponent Profile |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| One | Four | Emotionally manipulative, envious, undermines with feeling |
| Two | Eight | Dominating, controlling, takes rather than gives |
| Three | Nine | Passive-aggressive, undermines with inaction |
| Four | Two | Smotheringly helpful, exposes Four's hidden needs |
| Five | Seven | Scattered, demanding, invades privacy |
| Six | Three | Image-focused, untrustworthy, superficially confident |
| Seven | One | Rigidly critical, trapping, demands commitment |
| Eight | Five | Withdrawn, intellectually superior, refuses to engage |
| Nine | Six | Anxiously demanding, forces choices, creates conflict |

**Opponent Attack Strategy:**
1. Exploit blind spots (see what protagonist can't see about themselves)
2. Use distortion filters (communicate in ways that will be misinterpreted)
3. Press pinches (systematically trigger conflict buttons)
4. Block evolution (prevent access to safety/resources that allow growth)

## Lover/Romantic Partner Design

### Natural Attraction Patterns

Certain types frequently pair because they offer what the other lacks:

**High-Frequency Pairings:**
- **One + Seven:** Discipline meets spontaneity; each balances the other's extreme
- **Two + Eight:** The nurturer and protector; complementary forms of strength
- **Three + Six:** Achievement meets loyalty; image meets authenticity
- **Four + Nine:** Intensity meets calm; both seek depth differently
- **Five + Seven:** Depth meets breadth; wisdom meets joy
- **Eight + Nine:** Power meets peace; both Body types, different expressions

### What Each Type Seeks in Love

| Type | Seeks Partner Who... | Shows Love By... | Wounds Partner By... |
|------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------|
| One | Accepts their imperfections | Improving/helping them be better | Criticizing, withholding approval |
| Two | Needs them | Anticipating needs, giving | Smothering, creating guilt |
| Three | Admires them | Achieving for the relationship | Prioritizing image over intimacy |
| Four | Understands their depth | Emotional attunement, intensity | Push-pull, testing, drama |
| Five | Respects their space | Sharing knowledge, presence | Withdrawing, emotional unavailability |
| Six | Is reliably trustworthy | Loyalty, commitment, protection | Suspicion, testing, anxiety contagion |
| Seven | Won't trap them | Adventure, fun, positivity | Escaping, avoiding pain, leaving |
| Eight | Won't control or betray them | Protection, providing, directness | Domination, intimidation |
| Nine | Won't demand conflict | Accommodation, peace, presence | Disappearing, passive aggression |

### Romantic Conflict by Type Pairing

**Same-Type Pairings:** Intensified strengths AND weaknesses
- Two Ones: Escalating standards neither can meet
- Two Fours: Endless emotional processing, competition for suffering
- Two Eights: Explosive power struggles or perfect mutual respect

**Complementary Tensions:**
- One + Four: Structure vs. feeling; each triggers the other's de-evolution
- Two + Five: Connection vs. space; demand vs. withdrawal cycle
- Three + Four: Image vs. authenticity; success vs. meaning
- Six + Eight: Security through caution vs. security through power

## Mentor Design

Mentors should embody what the protagonist needs to learn—often representing a healthy version of the protagonist's own type or their evolution point.

### Mentor Archetypes by Protagonist Type

| Protagonist | Effective Mentor Type | What Mentor Models |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| One | Healthy One or Seven | Acceptance of imperfection; joy within discipline |
| Two | Healthy Two or Four | Self-care; receiving love without earning it |
| Three | Healthy Three or Six | Authentic value; success through connection |
| Four | Healthy Four or One | Creativity through discipline; belonging with uniqueness |
| Five | Healthy Five or Eight | Engaged mastery; knowledge in service of action |
| Six | Healthy Six or Nine | Inner authority; security from within |
| Seven | Healthy Seven or Five | Satisfaction in depth; presence over planning |
| Eight | Healthy Eight or Two | Strength through vulnerability; protection through love |
| Nine | Healthy Nine or Three | Presence with purpose; peace with engagement |

### Mentor Functions in Story

**False Mentor:** Appears helpful but enables protagonist's blind spot or represents de-evolution
**Threshold Guardian:** Tests protagonist using their pinches before allowing passage
**Shape-Shifter:** Mentor whose perceived type changes as protagonist's perception evolves

## Harmonic Triads (Conflict Styles)

How types handle adversity when needs aren't met:

**Positive Outlook (2, 7, 9):** Reframe negatives, find silver lining, avoid confrontation
**Reactive (4, 6, 8):** Emotional response, confrontation, need to be heard
**Competency (1, 3, 5):** Suppress emotions, focus on solutions, maintain composure

**Writing Application:** Characters from the same triad handle conflict similarly; mix triads for varied responses to the same crisis.

## Analyzing Existing Relationships

When analyzing a screenplay's relationships, ask:

1. **What type dynamics are at play?** Do characters represent opponent/ally/mentor patterns?
2. **What level is each character operating at?** Healthy characters can relate to any type; unhealthy pairings amplify dysfunction.
3. **Are relationship functions serving the protagonist's arc?** Does the opponent target blind spots? Does the ally model evolution?
4. **What's missing?** If protagonist has no character modeling their evolution point, who could serve that function?
5. **Are romantic tensions type-consistent?** Does the conflict between lovers match their Enneagram dynamics?
